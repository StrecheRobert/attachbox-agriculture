Smart Agriculture Projects

IoT, data platforms and digital tools for modern agricultural applications.

Overview

BEIA is involved in research and innovation activities that apply IoT technologies, communication systems and data platforms to the agricultural sector. These solutions are designed to monitor environmental and crop-related parameters, support precision agriculture and provide useful information for farmers and decision-makers.

The systems can integrate sensors, wireless communication, cloud databases, dashboards, alerting mechanisms and predictive components for better management of agricultural resources.

Example Agriculture Use Cases

1. Vineyard Monitoring

IoT stations can be deployed in vineyards to monitor parameters such as air temperature, humidity, soil moisture, solar radiation and other environmental indicators. The collected data can support disease risk detection, irrigation planning and crop management.

Viticulture Disease risk IoT sensors

2. Smart Irrigation Support

Soil and weather data can be used to support irrigation decisions. By monitoring soil moisture and environmental conditions, farmers can reduce water waste and improve resource efficiency.

Water management Soil moisture Decision support

3. Greenhouse Monitoring

In controlled environments such as greenhouses, sensors can monitor temperature, humidity, CO₂ concentration and light intensity. These data can help maintain optimal growing conditions and improve production quality.

Greenhouse Climate control Automation

4. Field Data Visualization

Data collected from agricultural sensors can be stored and visualized in dashboards. This allows users to follow trends, compare values over time and receive alerts when specific thresholds are exceeded.
